Henry Forda(1863–1947) was an American industrialist who founded Ford Motor Company and popularized assembly line production, making cars affordable. He also introduced the $5-a-day wage, improving worker conditions and productivity.

The first Ford factory, opened in 1903 in Detroit, produced the Model T. Its assembly line method revolutionized car manufacturing and mass production.

Ford's first engine, the 2-cylinder, 8-horsepower engine, was built in 1893. It powered his first self-built vehicle, the Quadricycle, which he drove in 1896. This engine marked the beginning of Ford's journey in automotive innovation.

The first Ford Mustang was introduced in 1964. It was a stylish, affordable sports car that quickly became an icon. With its sleek design and powerful engine options, the Mustang helped establish the "pony car" class and became a symbol of American automotive culture.

The Ford Mustang, launched in 1964, quickly became an icon in the automotive world, known for its stylish design and impressive performance. It was the first car to introduce the "pony car" segment, combining affordability with sporty features that appealed to a wide range of drivers. With its powerful engine options and sleek, aggressive look, the Mustang attracted attention and quickly became a symbol of American freedom and performance.
